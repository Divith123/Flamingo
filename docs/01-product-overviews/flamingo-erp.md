# Flamingo ERP - Enterprise Resource Planning

## Overview

Flamingo ERP is a comprehensive enterprise resource planning system designed for mid-market to enterprise organizations seeking to unify their business operations on a single, modern platform. It serves as the operational backbone of the Flamingo ecosystem, integrating financials, manufacturing, supply chain, human resources, customer relationship management, and business intelligence into one cohesive system. Unlike legacy ERP solutions that require years of implementation and massive consulting investments, Flamingo ERP delivers enterprise-grade functionality through a modular architecture that allows organizations to adopt capabilities incrementally while maintaining deep integration across all modules.

At its foundation, Flamingo ERP provides a single source of truth for business data, eliminating the silos and inconsistencies that plague organizations running disparate systems. Every transaction—from a manufacturing work order completion to a customer service case resolution—flows through unified data models that ensure consistency across financial reporting, operational dashboards, and compliance documentation. The platform's customizable workflows adapt to each organization's unique business processes rather than forcing operational changes to fit rigid software constraints.

The system's advanced analytics capabilities transform raw transaction data into actionable insights through real-time dashboards, predictive analytics, and AI-powered recommendations. Whether optimizing inventory levels based on demand forecasts, identifying production bottlenecks before they impact delivery schedules, or analyzing customer profitability across segments, Flamingo ERP provides the visibility needed for data-driven decision-making. Deep integration with the broader Flamingo ecosystem ensures seamless data flow between ERP core functions and specialized modules like Commerce, Billing, and Payroll, creating a unified business management platform that scales from growing mid-market companies to complex global enterprises.

## Market Analysis

### Market Landscape

The enterprise resource planning market represents one of the largest and most mature segments of enterprise software, with global revenues exceeding $50 billion annually. The market continues to evolve as organizations migrate from legacy on-premise systems to cloud-native solutions, driven by demands for greater flexibility, lower total cost of ownership, and enhanced mobility. Key trends shaping the ERP landscape include composable architecture allowing selective module adoption, AI-powered automation of routine business processes, embedded analytics providing real-time operational visibility, and industry-specific solutions tailored to vertical requirements.

Modern ERP buyers increasingly prioritize systems that can be implemented quickly without massive consulting engagements, integrate easily with existing technology stacks, and provide intuitive user experiences that drive adoption. The shift toward remote and hybrid work models has accelerated demand for cloud-based access, mobile capabilities, and collaborative features that connect distributed teams. Organizations are also seeking ERP platforms that can serve as integration hubs for specialized point solutions rather than requiring complete rip-and-replace implementations.

Flamingo ERP differentiates through:

5. **Mid-market focus**: Designed specifically for mid-market organizations that have outgrown entry-level tools but find enterprise ERP solutions prohibitively expensive and complex.

## Core Features

### 1. Financial Management

The Financial Management module provides comprehensive accounting capabilities that serve as the foundation for enterprise financial operations. The General Ledger supports multi-dimensional account structures with unlimited custom dimensions for analytical reporting across departments, projects, locations, product lines, and any business segmentation without cluttering the chart of accounts. Account hierarchies enable roll-up reporting while maintaining transaction-level detail, with flexible numbering schemes supporting any organizational convention.

Accounts Payable manages the complete vendor payment lifecycle from invoice receipt through disbursement. Electronic data interchange (EDI) imports structured invoice data directly, while email capture extracts data from PDF attachments using OCR technology. Three-way matching validates invoice amounts against purchase orders and receiving documents, with configurable variance tolerances. Payment scheduling optimizes cash flow while capturing early payment discounts, supporting ACH transfers, wire transfers, virtual credit cards, and check printing.

Accounts Receivable accelerates cash collection through automated invoice generation, intelligent payment matching, and collections workflow management. Credit management features track payment history, calculate credit scores, and monitor exposure against limits. Revenue recognition capabilities ensure compliance with accounting standards through support for point-in-time recognition, percentage-of-completion, milestone-based, and subscription revenue recognition schedules.

Fixed Assets maintains complete asset registers with automated depreciation calculations using straight-line, declining balance, sum-of-years-digits, or units-of-production methods. Asset tracking includes acquisition details, maintenance history, impairment assessments, and disposal processing with gain/loss calculations. Integration with procurement automatically creates asset records from capital purchase receipts.

Cash Management provides comprehensive treasury capabilities including bank feed integration with thousands of financial institutions, intelligent transaction matching, and cash flow forecasting. Multi-currency support includes real-time exchange rate feeds, comprehensive realized and unrealized gain/loss tracking, and currency translation for foreign operations. Cash position dashboards provide real-time visibility into liquidity across all accounts and currencies.

### 2. Manufacturing

The Manufacturing module supports discrete, process, and mixed-mode manufacturing with comprehensive production planning and execution capabilities. Bill of Materials (BOM) management maintains multi-level product structures with component quantities, reference designators, and substitute materials. Revision control tracks engineering changes with effective dates and approval workflows. Where-used inquiries identify all products affected by component changes for impact assessment.

Work Order Management creates production orders from MRP recommendations, sales order demand, or manual entry. Work orders specify the product, quantity, BOM revision, routing, and scheduled start/completion dates. Component allocation reserves inventory for production while maintaining availability visibility. Backflushing automatically consumes components based on BOM quantities upon work order completion.

Shop Floor Control tracks production progress through operation sequences with labor and machine time collection. Operator dashboards display current work assignments with setup instructions, quality checkpoints, and completion confirmation. Real-time production reporting compares actual to standard times for efficiency analysis. Work center calendars define available capacity considering shifts, maintenance schedules, and holidays.

Quality Control enforces inspection requirements at receiving, in-process, and final inspection points. Inspection plans define sample sizes, acceptance criteria, and measurement characteristics. Non-conformance tracking documents quality failures with containment actions, root cause analysis, and corrective/preventive action (CAPA) workflows. Quality metrics including first-pass yield, defect rates, and supplier quality scores drive continuous improvement.

Capacity Planning balances production requirements with available resources through finite or infinite scheduling algorithms. Load profiles visualize work center utilization over time, identifying bottlenecks and underutilized resources. What-if simulations model the impact of schedule changes, overtime, or outsourcing decisions. Constraint-based scheduling optimizes sequences considering setup minimization, priority rules, and resource availability.

### 3. Supply Chain

The Supply Chain module optimizes the flow of materials from suppliers through production to customers with comprehensive procurement, inventory, warehousing, and logistics capabilities. Procurement automates purchasing workflows from requisition through payment, supporting strategic sourcing initiatives and supplier relationship management. Requisition workflows route purchase requests through approval hierarchies based on amount, category, and department. Purchase orders generate from MRP suggestions, reorder point triggers, or manual entry with supplier-specific pricing and terms.

Inventory Management provides real-time visibility into stock levels across multiple locations with support for serial numbers, lot numbers, and expiry date tracking. Allocation strategies reserve inventory for specific demand while maintaining flexibility for reallocation. Available-to-promise (ATP) calculations provide accurate delivery commitments considering on-hand inventory, scheduled receipts, and existing reservations. Inventory valuation supports FIFO, LIFO, weighted average, and standard cost methods with automatic COGS posting.

Warehouse Management optimizes storage organization and operational workflows with directed put-away, pick path optimization, and cycle counting capabilities. Location hierarchies model physical warehouses with zones, aisles, bays, and bins. Put-away algorithms suggest optimal storage based on product velocity, dimensions, and storage requirements. Picking workflows support discrete, batch, wave, and zone picking strategies with mobile scanning confirmation.

Logistics Coordination manages transportation planning, carrier selection, and shipment tracking. Route optimization consolidates shipments considering delivery windows, vehicle capacity, and cost constraints. Carrier integration provides real-time rate shopping, shipping label generation, and tracking number assignment. Delivery confirmation triggers customer notifications and revenue recognition.

Demand Planning forecasts future requirements using statistical models, sales projections, and collaborative input. Time-series analysis identifies trends, seasonality, and cyclical patterns in historical demand. Forecast accuracy measurements enable continuous model refinement. Collaborative planning workflows incorporate sales, marketing, and customer input for promotional and new product forecasts.

### 4. Project Management

The Project Management module supports project-based organizations with comprehensive planning, execution, and financial management capabilities. Project Planning creates work breakdown structures (WBS) defining project scope through hierarchical task decomposition. Task dependencies establish predecessor-successor relationships that drive schedule calculations. Critical path analysis identifies tasks that directly impact project completion dates. Resource leveling resolves overallocation by adjusting schedules or suggesting additional resources.

Resource Management maintains resource pools with skills, availability, and cost rates. Resource loading views display assignments across projects, identifying overallocation and availability gaps. Skills matrices match resource capabilities to project requirements. Resource forecasting projects future demand based on planned projects and resource supply based on hiring plans.

Time Tracking captures actual effort against project tasks through timesheets with approval workflows. Task-level time entry provides granular visibility into work performed. Billable time flags identify hours eligible for customer invoicing. Time validation enforces charge codes, project budgets, and approval requirements. Mobile time entry enables field staff to report time from any location.

Project Billing generates customer invoices based on contract terms including time and materials, fixed price, milestone-based, or recurring billing arrangements. Progress billing recognizes revenue based on percentage completion or milestone achievement. Retainage tracking manages withheld amounts pending project acceptance. Interim billing provides cash flow for long-duration projects.

Project Costing accumulates all project costs including labor, materials, subcontractor services, and overhead allocations. Budget versus actual comparisons highlight variances requiring attention. Estimate to complete (ETC) projections forecast final costs based on remaining work. Project profitability analysis compares revenue to total costs including overhead allocations.

## User Stories

### Story 1: CFO Implements Financial Management Module

**As a** Chief Financial Officer, **I want to** implement the financial management module with multi-entity consolidation, **so that** we can streamline our financial operations and produce accurate consolidated reports.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- CFO defines entity hierarchy with parent company and three subsidiaries.
- Chart of accounts is configured with multi-dimensional tracking for departments and projects.
- Intercompany accounts are designated for automatic elimination.
- Opening balances are entered and validated for all entities.
- Period-end close workflow is configured with task checklists and approval requirements.
- Consolidation processing eliminates intercompany transactions and translates foreign currencies.
- Financial statements generate for individual entities and consolidated group.
- Audit trail documents all setup activities and configuration changes.

### Story 2: Operations Manager Configures Manufacturing Workflows

**As an** Operations Manager, **I want to** configure manufacturing workflows including BOMs, routings, and work centers, **so that** we can track production costs and manage shop floor operations.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Manager creates bill of materials for finished goods with component quantities and references.
- Routing definitions specify operation sequences, work centers, and standard times.
- Work centers are configured with capacity calendars and cost rates.
- Work order creation generates from MRP suggestions or sales order demand.
- Component allocation reserves inventory for production orders.
- Shop floor operators record labor and machine time against operations.
- Work order completion triggers inventory receipt and cost calculation.
- Variance analysis compares actual to standard costs for continuous improvement.

### Story 3: Supply Chain Director Sets Up Procurement Automation

**As a** Supply Chain Director, **I want to** configure procurement automation with approval workflows and supplier management, **so that** we can optimize purchasing processes and control costs.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Director configures approval workflows routing purchase orders based on amount thresholds.
- Supplier master data includes contact information, payment terms, and performance history.
- Reorder points and order quantities are calculated based on demand history and lead times.
- MRP generates purchase requisitions for materials below reorder points.
- Purchase orders route through approval workflows with email notifications.
- Receiving workflows confirm deliveries with three-way matching to invoices.
- Supplier scorecards track on-time delivery, quality acceptance, and price variance.
- Procurement dashboards display open orders, spending by category, and savings metrics.

### Story 4: Controller Runs Consolidated Financial Reports

**As a** Corporate Controller, **I want to** run consolidated financial reports across all subsidiaries, **so that** I can present accurate group financial results to stakeholders.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Controller initiates consolidation process from the financial closing workspace.
- System translates foreign subsidiary results to functional currency using period-end rates.

- Consolidated balance sheet, income statement, and cash flow generate automatically.
- Elimination detail reports show all intercompany transactions removed.

**As a** Global Operations VP, **I want to** configure the ERP for multi-country operations with localized compliance, **so that** we can support international expansion while maintaining regulatory compliance.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

## Integration Points

### Flamingo Books Integration

Flamingo ERP serves as the central hub for financial data flowing from all Flamingo products, with Books providing the core accounting foundation:

- **Chart of Accounts Synchronization**: The ERP chart of accounts serves as the master for all Flamingo products, ensuring consistent financial classification across Expense, Payroll, Billing, and Commerce transactions. Account structures, dimensions, and hierarchies propagate automatically.

- **Automatic Journal Entry Generation**: Transactions from all ERP modules generate journal entries in Books automatically. Manufacturing completions post WIP to finished goods transfers, procurement receipts create inventory and liability entries, and project billing generates revenue recognition entries.

- **Consolidation Hub**: ERP consolidates financial data from all Flamingo modules for organizations using specialized modules at subsidiaries. Books data feeds into ERP consolidation engines for group reporting.

- **Budget Integration**: ERP budgets flow into Books for variance analysis, while actuals from Books feed ERP analytics for performance measurement.

### Flamingo Inventory Integration

Inventory management connects deeply with ERP manufacturing and supply chain functions:

- **Manufacturing Material Flow**: ERP work orders consume Inventory raw materials and produce finished goods with automatic inventory transactions. Backflushing deducts components based on BOM quantities upon completion.

- **Procurement Coordination**: ERP purchase requisitions generate Inventory purchase orders with three-way matching between requisition, receipt, and invoice.

- **Multi-Location Visibility**: ERP provides consolidated inventory visibility across all Inventory-managed locations with inter-warehouse transfer coordination.

- **Valuation Integration**: Inventory valuation methods (FIFO, LIFO, average cost) align with ERP financial posting, ensuring COGS accuracy.

### Flamingo Payroll Integration

HR management in ERP coordinates closely with Payroll processing:

- **Employee Master Sync**: Employee records created in ERP HR automatically provision in Payroll with organizational attributes, compensation data, and deduction elections.

- **Time and Attendance**: ERP time tracking captures employee hours, absence requests, and leave balances that flow to Payroll for gross pay calculation.

- **Benefits Coordination**: ERP benefits administration manages enrollments and elections that determine Payroll deductions and employer contributions.

- **Cost Allocation**: Payroll costs allocate to ERP dimensions (department, project, cost center) for accurate cost accounting and project costing.

### Flamingo Procurement Integration

Supply chain functions integrate bidirectionally with Procurement:

- **Requisition to Purchase Order**: Procurement requisitions approved in the Procurement module convert to ERP purchase orders with supplier, product, and quantity details.

- **Supplier Management**: Supplier master data synchronizes between systems with performance metrics, pricing agreements, and contact information.

- **Three-Way Matching**: ERP receiving confirmations match against Procurement purchase orders and Accounts Payable invoices for payment authorization.

- **Spend Analysis**: Procurement spend data feeds ERP analytics for supplier performance measurement and sourcing decisions.

### Flamingo Billing Integration

Revenue management connects subscription billing with ERP financials:

- **Revenue Recognition**: Billing subscription schedules inform ERP revenue recognition calculations, ensuring ASC 606 compliance with automated monthly entries.

- **Customer Lifecycle**: Customer status changes in Billing (trial, active, churned) reflect in ERP CRM for unified customer analytics.

- **Invoice Generation**: Billing charges automatically generate ERP accounts receivable records with proper GL account coding.

- **Payment Application**: Payments processed through Billing update ERP receivables and cash management with automatic gain/loss calculation for foreign currency.

### Flamingo Commerce Integration

E-commerce operations integrate with ERP supply chain and CRM:

- **Order-to-Cash Flow**: Commerce orders flow into ERP as sales orders with automatic inventory allocation and fulfillment workflow initiation.

- **Customer Data Synchronization**: Commerce customers synchronize with ERP CRM for unified relationship management and cross-channel analytics.

- **Inventory Availability**: Real-time ERP inventory feeds Commerce product availability, preventing oversells while considering safety stock and allocations.

- **Product Master**: Products created in ERP propagate to Commerce with attributes, pricing, and inventory levels.

### Cross-Product Workflow Orchestration

ERP orchestrates complex workflows spanning multiple Flamingo products:

- **Quote-to-Cash**: Commerce quotes convert to orders, generate invoices in Billing, create receivables in Books, and recognize revenue in ERP, with status synchronized across all modules.

- **Procure-to-Pay**: Procurement requisitions create purchase orders, receive inventory, match invoices, and schedule payments with workflow status visible throughout.

- **Hire-to-Retire**: Employee onboarding in ERP triggers Payroll setup, benefit enrollments, system provisioning, and training assignments with coordinated status tracking.

- **Plan-to-Produce**: ERP demand planning generates procurement recommendations and manufacturing orders, coordinating Procurement, Inventory, and production workflows.

## Technical Considerations

### Multi-Tenant Architecture

Flamingo ERP implements true multi-tenancy with logical data isolation ensuring security and scalability:

- **Tenant Isolation**: Each organization's data is strictly isolated at the database level using row-level security policies. Queries automatically filter by tenant context, preventing cross-tenant data access.

- **Configurable Tenancy**: Organizations can choose shared database tenancy for cost efficiency or dedicated database resources for performance isolation and regulatory compliance.

- **Customization Isolation**: Tenant-specific customizations including fields, workflows, and reports are isolated and do not affect other tenants. Customization metadata is tenant-scoped.

- **Resource Scaling**: Compute and storage resources scale elastically based on tenant size and activity patterns, ensuring consistent performance without over-provisioning.

### Customization Flexibility

The platform provides extensive customization capabilities while maintaining upgrade compatibility:

- **Configuration-First Approach**: Business requirements are met through configuration rather than code modification, ensuring customizations survive platform upgrades.

- **Extension Points**: Well-defined extension points allow tenant-specific logic including custom validations, calculated fields, and workflow actions without core code changes.

- **Versioning**: Customizations are versioned with change tracking, enabling rollback to previous configurations and audit of modification history.

- **Sandbox Testing**: Configuration changes can be tested in isolated sandbox environments before promotion to production, reducing risk of disruption.

### Data Migration Tools

Comprehensive tools support migration from legacy systems with minimal disruption:

- **Import Templates**: Predefined templates for common data types (customers, vendors, products, chart of accounts) ensure consistent data formatting.

- **Transformation Engine**: Built-in transformation rules convert data formats, map codes, and calculate derived values during import.

- **Validation Engine**: Data validation identifies errors and inconsistencies before import, with detailed error reporting for correction.

- **Incremental Migration**: Supports phased migration approaches where historical data is imported separately from active transactions.

- **Reconciliation Reports**: Post-migration reconciliation validates data completeness and accuracy, comparing source system totals to imported data.

### Security Architecture

Defense-in-depth security protects sensitive ERP data:

- **Authentication**: Better Auth provides comprehensive authentication with SSO, multi-factor authentication, and session management. Integration with corporate identity providers enables centralized access control.

- **Authorization**: Role-based access control (RBAC) and attribute-based access control (ABAC) enforce least-privilege access. Permissions can be scoped to entities, departments, or custom dimensions.

- **Data Encryption**: AES-256 encryption protects data at rest with tenant-specific encryption keys. TLS 1.3 ensures encryption in transit.

- **Audit Logging**: Immutable audit logs capture all data access and modifications with cryptographic verification preventing tampering.

- **Compliance Certification**: SOC 2 Type II certification validates security controls. GDPR compliance features support data protection requirements.

### Scalability and Performance

Architecture designed for enterprise-scale transaction volumes:

- **Horizontal Scaling**: Stateless application tier scales horizontally through container orchestration. Load balancers distribute traffic with health-based routing.

- **Database Optimization**: Read replicas handle analytical queries while primary databases manage transactions. Connection pooling and query optimization ensure sub-second response times.

- **Caching Strategy**: Multi-tier caching reduces database load with intelligent invalidation ensuring data consistency.

- **Asynchronous Processing**: Background job queues handle long-running processes including report generation, data imports, and workflow automation without blocking user interactions.

- **Performance Monitoring**: Comprehensive observability tracks response times, throughput, and resource utilization with automated alerting for degradation.

### Integration Architecture

API-first design enables seamless ecosystem connectivity:

- **REST APIs**: Standard RESTful APIs with OpenAPI documentation enable integration with any system supporting HTTP.

- **tRPC**: Type-safe remote procedures provide enhanced developer experience for TypeScript integrations with automatic client generation.

- **Event Architecture**: Event-driven integration using BullMQ message queues enables asynchronous, decoupled communication between systems.

- **Webhook Support**: Real-time event notifications to external systems with retry logic and delivery confirmation.

- **Pre-built Connectors**: Library of connectors for popular systems including Salesforce, Shopify, Amazon, and major banks.
