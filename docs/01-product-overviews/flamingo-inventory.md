# Flamingo Inventory - Stock and Inventory Management

## Overview

Flamingo Inventory is a comprehensive stock and inventory management module within the Flamingo business ecosystem. It provides organizations with complete visibility and control over their inventory across multiple locations, warehouses, and sales channels. The module handles the entire inventory lifecycle from product creation and purchase order receiving through sales order fulfillment and stock valuation, enabling businesses to optimize stock levels, reduce carrying costs, and improve order fulfillment accuracy.

The module integrates seamlessly with Flamingo Commerce for real-time product synchronization and order fulfillment, Flamingo Procurement for purchase order management and supplier coordination, Flamingo Books for automated cost of goods sold (COGS) tracking and inventory valuation, and Flamingo ERP for manufacturing planning and production scheduling. This native ecosystem integration eliminates data silos and ensures that inventory data flows consistently across all business operations.

Flamingo Inventory targets mid-market and enterprise organizations in retail, wholesale, manufacturing, and e-commerce sectors that require multi-location inventory visibility, sophisticated warehouse management capabilities, and tight integration with their financial systems. The module supports businesses with complex inventory needs including serialized products, lot tracking, expiry date management, and multi-level bill of materials for manufacturing operations.

The core value proposition centers on four pillars: real-time visibility (accurate stock levels across all locations updated instantaneously), operational efficiency (streamlined receiving, picking, and fulfillment workflows), financial accuracy (automated inventory valuation and COGS posting), and scalability (support for high-volume operations across distributed warehouse networks).

## Market Analysis

### Market Landscape

The inventory management software market is projected to exceed $5 billion by 2028, driven by e-commerce growth, supply chain complexity, and the need for real-time visibility into stock levels. Organizations are moving from spreadsheet-based and legacy ERP inventory systems toward cloud-native solutions that provide mobile accessibility, barcode scanning integration, and API-first architectures for system integration.

Key market trends shaping product development include:

- **Real-time inventory visibility**: Customers expect accurate stock availability across all channels, requiring sub-second inventory updates as transactions occur.
- **Omnichannel fulfillment**: Businesses need to fulfill orders from multiple inventory locations (warehouses, stores, drop-ship partners) with intelligent sourcing algorithms.
- **Mobile warehouse operations**: Warehouse staff increasingly use mobile devices and handheld scanners for receiving, picking, and cycle counting operations.
- **AI-powered demand forecasting**: Machine learning models analyze historical sales, seasonality, and external factors to predict optimal stock levels and reorder points.
- **IoT integration**: RFID tags, smart shelves, and connected devices provide automated inventory tracking without manual scanning.

## Core Features

### 1. Product Catalog

The product catalog provides a centralized repository for all inventory items with flexible configuration options to support diverse business requirements:

- **SKU management**: Each product is assigned a unique stock keeping unit (SKU) that serves as the primary identifier across all inventory operations. The system supports automatic SKU generation based on configurable rules (category prefix + sequential number) or manual entry. SKU formats can include alphanumeric characters, dashes, and underscores with configurable length limits. Products can have alternate SKUs for cross-referencing supplier part numbers or legacy system identifiers.

- **Product variants**: Configurable product options support size, color, material, and custom variant dimensions. Each variant combination generates a unique SKU with its own stock tracking while sharing common product attributes like descriptions, images, and categorization. Variant templates allow rapid creation of new products with predefined option sets. Matrix views display all variant combinations with stock levels for quick inventory assessment.

- **Product bundles**: Virtual products composed of multiple component items can be created without physical pre-packaging. Bundle pricing can be set as fixed amount or component sum with configurable markup or discount. During order fulfillment, bundle components are automatically deducted from inventory with visibility into component availability before bundle sale confirmation. Assembly workflows support physical kitting operations when pre-packaged bundles are preferred.

- **Categorization and attributes**: Hierarchical category structures organize products with up to five category levels. Custom attribute definitions support text, number, date, boolean, and dropdown data types for product specification capture. Attribute sets can be defined by category to ensure consistent data entry. Product tags provide flexible cross-category grouping for reporting and filtering purposes.

- **Unit of measure**: Products can be tracked in multiple units of measure with conversion factors (e.g., each, case of 12, pallet of 48). Primary stockkeeping units define the base unit for quantity tracking while secondary units support purchasing in bulk and selling in individual quantities. Unit conversions are applied automatically during receiving and order fulfillment operations.

- **Product images and documents**: Multiple product images can be uploaded with configurable display order and alt text. Document attachments include specification sheets, safety data sheets, certificates of analysis, and supplier documentation. Images and documents are accessible during receiving, picking, and customer service operations.

### 2. Stock Management

Stock management provides real-time visibility and control over inventory quantities across all locations:

- **Quantity tracking**: The system maintains multiple quantity fields for each product-location combination: on-hand (physical quantity in location), allocated (reserved for sales orders), available (on-hand minus allocated), on-order (quantity on open purchase orders), and in-transit (quantity in transfer between locations). Quantity updates occur in real-time as transactions are processed, ensuring accurate availability for sales order promising.

- **Stock level alerts**: Configurable reorder points and maximum stock levels trigger automatic alerts when inventory falls below or exceeds thresholds. Alert notifications can be sent via email, in-app notification, or webhook to external systems. Reorder point calculations can be manual entry or automatically calculated based on historical demand, lead times, and safety stock formulas.

- **Stock adjustments**: Manual adjustments correct inventory discrepancies identified during cycle counts or physical audits. Adjustment reasons are required (damage, expiry, theft, found, count correction) with optional notes for audit trail. Adjustment approvals can be configured to require manager authorization based on quantity or value thresholds.

- **Negative stock control**: Organizations can configure whether negative stock is permitted by location and product category. When negative stock is blocked, sales orders cannot be confirmed if allocation would result in negative available quantity. Backorder management tracks unfulfilled demand with automatic fulfillment when stock becomes available.

- **Serial number tracking**: Serialized products maintain individual unit records with unique serial numbers tracked from receipt through sale. Serial number history includes receipt date, purchase order, supplier, warehouse movements, sales order, and customer. This enables complete traceability for warranty, recall, and regulatory compliance purposes.

- **Lot tracking and expiry**: Lot-tracked products maintain batch-level inventory with lot numbers assigned at receipt. Expiry dates are tracked for perishable goods with first-expiry-first-out (FEFO) picking support. Quarantine status prevents expired or suspect lots from being allocated to orders. Lot recall workflows identify all customers who received specific lot numbers for rapid notification.

### 3. Warehouse Management

Warehouse management optimizes storage organization and operational workflows within distribution facilities:

- **Location hierarchy**: Physical warehouses are modeled with hierarchical location structures: warehouse > zone > aisle > bay > shelf > bin. Location types define storage characteristics (bulk, pick face, refrigerated, hazardous, quarantine) with capacity constraints and product restrictions. Location status (active, inactive, blocked) controls availability for put-away and picking operations.

- **Bin management**: Individual storage locations (bins) have defined dimensions, weight capacity, and product restrictions. Multiple products can share bins if configured, or bins can be dedicated to single products for high-velocity items. Bin assignments recommend optimal storage locations based on product velocity, size, and storage requirements.

- **Put-away workflows**: Directed put-away suggests optimal storage locations based on product dimensions, velocity, and storage requirements. Put-away tasks are generated upon purchase order receipt and can be executed via mobile scanning for confirmation. Exceptions are flagged when suggested locations are unavailable or product conditions require inspection.

- **Picking optimization**: Pick lists are organized by pick path sequence to minimize warehouse travel time. Wave picking groups orders by zone or route for efficient batch processing. Batch picking allows single trips to collect items for multiple orders simultaneously. Pick priority rules ensure time-sensitive orders are processed first.

- **Packing and shipping**: Packing stations verify picked items against order requirements with weight validation. Packing slip and shipping label generation integrates with carrier systems for rate shopping and tracking number assignment. Package dimensions and weights are captured for shipping cost calculation and carrier manifest generation.

- **Cross-docking**: Cross-dock workflows route incoming inventory directly to outbound staging without put-away when purchase orders are linked to sales orders. This reduces handling costs and delivery times for drop-ship and just-in-time scenarios.

### 4. Purchase Orders

Purchase order management streamlines procurement and receiving workflows:

- **PO creation**: Purchase orders are created manually, generated from reorder suggestions, or converted from procurement requisitions. Supplier selection presents preferred vendors with pricing history and lead time information. Line items support multiple units of measure with automatic conversion to stockkeeping units. Tax calculations, freight estimates, and discount applications provide total landed cost visibility.

- **Approval workflows**: Configurable approval rules route purchase orders based on amount, supplier, category, or department. Multi-level approvals support department manager, finance, and executive authorization levels. Approval notifications include line-item detail with one-click approval from email or mobile application.

- **Receiving workflows**: Partial and full receipt capabilities handle supplier shipments that may span multiple deliveries. Blind receiving conceals expected quantities to encourage accurate counting. Mobile scanning confirms receipt by SKU with quantity entry and lot/serial capture as required. Discrepancies trigger exception workflows for quantity variances, damage, or quality issues.

- **Three-way matching**: Purchase orders, receipts, and supplier invoices are matched automatically to validate payment authorization. Variance tolerances allow minor discrepancies to pass while flagging significant differences for review. Matched invoices flow to accounts payable for payment processing.

- **Supplier performance**: Delivery performance metrics track on-time delivery rates, quantity accuracy, and quality acceptance rates. Lead time analysis identifies suppliers with consistent delivery windows for planning purposes. Purchase history provides negotiation support with spend analysis by supplier and product.

- **Returns management**: Return merchandise authorization (RMA) workflows document supplier returns with reason codes and expected credit amounts. Return tracking monitors credit memo receipt and ensures proper financial reconciliation.

### 5. Sales Orders

Sales order management handles customer order processing from entry through fulfillment:

- **Order entry**: Orders are created manually, imported via EDI/API, or received from integrated e-commerce platforms. Customer-specific pricing and discount rules apply automatically based on contract terms. Product availability checks confirm stock allocation at order entry with backorder options for out-of-stock items.

- **Allocation management**: Inventory allocation reserves stock for specific sales orders, preventing oversell while maintaining flexibility to reallocate based on priority. Allocation strategies include strict FIFO, customer priority, order date, or promised ship date. Available-to-promise (ATP) calculations provide future availability dates based on incoming purchase orders and production.

- **Pick and pack**: Pick lists are generated automatically upon order confirmation with optimization for warehouse efficiency. Pick confirmation via mobile scanning validates item and quantity with lot/serial capture as required. Packing verification confirms complete order assembly before shipping label generation.

- **Shipment management**: Multiple shipments per order support partial fulfillment and split shipments from different warehouses. Carrier integration provides real-time rate shopping, label printing, and tracking number assignment. Delivery confirmation updates order status and triggers customer notifications.

- **Order tracking**: Customer-facing order status portals display order confirmation, allocation, picking, packing, and shipping milestones. Internal tracking provides visibility into exceptions, delays, and holds requiring intervention.

- **Returns and exchanges**: Return authorization workflows document customer returns with reason codes and expected disposition (restock, dispose, return to supplier). Restock put-away returns sellable inventory to available stock with quality inspection requirements.

### 6. Inventory Valuation

Inventory valuation provides accurate financial reporting and cost of goods sold calculation:

- **FIFO (First In, First Out)**: Assumes oldest inventory is sold first, matching current costs to revenue during inflationary periods. Layer tracking maintains separate cost layers for each receipt with automatic consumption from oldest layers first. FIFO is appropriate for perishable goods and businesses experiencing rising costs.

- **LIFO (Last In, First Out)**: Assumes newest inventory is sold first, matching current costs to current revenue for tax advantages in some jurisdictions. Layer tracking maintains cost layers with consumption from newest receipts first. LIFO provides tax benefits during inflation but may result in undervalued inventory on balance sheets.

- **Weighted Average**: Calculates average cost per unit by dividing total inventory value by total quantity on hand. New receipts update the average cost with weighted blending of existing and new inventory values. Weighted average smooths cost fluctuations and simplifies record-keeping for homogeneous products.

- **Standard Cost**: Uses predetermined standard costs for inventory valuation with variance tracking for purchase price differences. Purchase price variances (PPV) are recorded separately when actual costs differ from standards. Standard costing facilitates budgeting and performance measurement but requires periodic standard updates.

- **COGS posting**: Cost of goods sold is calculated automatically upon sales order shipment based on the selected valuation method. Journal entries post to the general ledger in Flamingo Books with debit to COGS and credit to inventory asset accounts. Period-end inventory valuation reports support financial statement preparation and audit requirements.

- **Inventory adjustments**: Adjustments to inventory quantities are valued at the current cost basis for the affected products. Physical count variances post to inventory adjustment accounts for investigation and approval. Obsolescence and write-downs reduce inventory value with corresponding expense recognition.

### 7. Reorder Management

Reorder management optimizes stock levels to balance availability against carrying costs:

- **Reorder point calculation**: Reorder points can be manually specified or automatically calculated using the formula: (Average Daily Demand × Lead Time in Days) + Safety Stock. Demand calculations use configurable historical periods (e.g., last 90 days) with seasonal adjustment factors. Lead times are tracked per supplier with automatic updates based on actual delivery performance.

- **Safety stock optimization**: Safety stock levels protect against demand variability and supply uncertainty. Statistical calculations use standard deviation of demand and lead times to achieve target service levels (e.g., 95% fill rate). Manual override allows business judgment for critical or seasonal items.

- **Reorder suggestions**: The system generates reorder suggestions when projected inventory falls below reorder points. Suggestion reports include recommended quantity (reorder point + order quantity − available), supplier, expected cost, and estimated delivery date. Suggestions can be converted to purchase orders individually or in batch.

- **Economic order quantity**: EOQ calculations recommend optimal order quantities that minimize total inventory costs (ordering costs plus carrying costs). The EOQ formula considers annual demand, ordering cost per purchase order, and carrying cost percentage. Results are advisory with manual override capability.

- **ABC analysis**: Products are classified by annual usage value into A (high value, tight control), B (medium value, standard control), and C (low value, simple control) categories. Different reorder policies can be applied by ABC class, such as weekly review for A items and monthly review for C items.

- **Supplier lead time tracking**: Actual delivery performance is tracked against promised dates with average lead time calculations by supplier-product combination. Lead time variability is factored into safety stock calculations for critical items.

### 8. Barcode and RFID

Barcode and RFID capabilities enable efficient data capture throughout warehouse operations:

- **Barcode symbology support**: The system supports common barcode formats including Code 128, Code 39, UPC-A, UPC-E, EAN-13, EAN-8, and QR codes. Product labels, location labels, and transaction documents can be printed with appropriate symbologies. Barcode label templates are customizable with organization branding and required data elements.

- **Mobile scanning applications**: Native iOS and Android applications provide barcode scanning using device cameras or connected Bluetooth scanners. Offline operation queues transactions for synchronization when connectivity is restored, enabling continuous operation in areas with poor network coverage. Scan validation confirms correct item, location, or order with audio and visual feedback.

- **Receiving by scan**: Purchase order receipts are confirmed by scanning product barcodes with quantity verification against expected counts. Lot numbers and serial numbers are captured during receipt scanning with validation against supplier documentation. Exceptions are flagged for quantity discrepancies, unexpected items, or quality concerns.

- **Pick and pack by scan**: Picking operations confirm item selection by scanning product and location barcodes. Serial and lot numbers are captured during picking for traceable products. Packing scans verify complete order assembly before shipment confirmation.

- **Cycle count by scan**: Physical inventory counts are performed using mobile scanning for rapid data entry. Count variance reports highlight discrepancies requiring investigation and adjustment. Count history tracks who counted, when, and what adjustments were made.

- **RFID integration**: RFID tag reading supports bulk inventory operations without line-of-sight scanning. RFID portals track inventory movement through dock doors and zone transitions. RFID cycle counting enables rapid inventory verification by reading entire shelves or containers simultaneously.

### 9. Stock Transfers

Stock transfer management handles inventory movement between locations:

- **Inter-warehouse transfers**: Inventory can be moved between warehouses to balance stock levels or fulfill orders from optimal locations. Transfer orders document the movement with source location, destination location, products, quantities, and requested/ship/receive dates. Transfer status tracks progress from creation through shipment to receipt.

- **Transfer workflows**: Transfer requests can be created manually or generated automatically based on reorder points at destination with availability at source. Picking at the source warehouse follows standard warehouse management workflows. Shipment confirmation reduces source location inventory and creates in-transit inventory records.

- **In-transit tracking**: Inventory in transit is tracked separately from on-hand stock with visibility into expected receipt dates and carrier tracking information. In-transit quantities are considered in available-to-promise calculations with configurable inclusion rules.

- **Transfer receipt**: Destination warehouse receipts confirm quantities received with variance tracking for loss or damage during transit. Put-away at destination follows standard receiving workflows with directed storage location suggestions.

- **Store transfers**: Retail organizations can transfer inventory between store locations to fulfill customer requests or balance slow-moving stock. Store transfer workflows include point-of-sale integration for inventory updates and customer notification when transfers arrive.

- **Transfer cost allocation**: Transfer costs (freight, handling) can be allocated to the inventory value for accurate landed cost calculation or recorded separately as operating expenses. Cost allocation methods include equal distribution across line items or weighted by product value.

### 10. Cycle Counting

Cycle counting provides continuous inventory verification without disrupting operations:

- **Count schedule generation**: Count schedules are generated based on ABC classification, with A items counted most frequently (e.g., monthly), B items less frequently (e.g., quarterly), and C items least frequently (e.g., annually). Random selection ensures unpredictable count timing to prevent manipulation. Zone-based counting schedules all items in specific warehouse areas on designated days.

- **Count task assignment**: Count tasks are assigned to warehouse personnel with mobile access to count sheets. Tasks include location, product, expected quantity, and unit of measure. Counts can be blind (expected quantity hidden) or open depending on organizational policy.

- **Count execution**: Mobile scanning or manual entry records actual quantities counted. First and second counts can be required for high-value items or when initial variance exceeds thresholds. Count notes document conditions affecting accuracy (damaged packaging, mixed products).

- **Variance analysis**: Count variance reports compare actual to expected quantities with value impact calculation. Root cause analysis identifies patterns (systematic errors, process gaps, theft) requiring corrective action. Inventory adjustments post after variance review and approval.

- **Count accuracy metrics**: Accuracy rates are tracked by product, location, and counter to identify training needs or process improvements. Metrics include count accuracy percentage, dollar variance as percentage of total inventory value, and trends over time.

- **Freeze and control**: Locations can be frozen during counting to prevent transactions that would create variances. System-controlled freeze automatically prevents put-away, picking, and adjustments in active count locations.

### 11. Reporting and Analytics

Reporting and analytics provide visibility into inventory performance and trends:

- **Stock level reports**: Current inventory reports display on-hand, allocated, available, and on-order quantities by product and location. Stock status summaries highlight items below reorder point, excess inventory, and inactive stock. Aging reports identify slow-moving and obsolete inventory requiring markdown or disposal.

- **Inventory valuation reports**: Valuation reports calculate inventory value by location, category, and product using the selected valuation method. Layer detail reports show cost layers for FIFO and LIFO valuation with quantity and value by layer. Period comparison reports track inventory value changes over time.

- **Turnover analysis**: Inventory turnover ratios calculate cost of goods sold divided by average inventory value, indicating how efficiently inventory is being managed. Days sales of inventory (DSI) shows average days to convert inventory to sales. Turnover benchmarks by category support performance comparison.

- **Demand forecasting**: Historical sales analysis identifies trends, seasonality, and growth patterns. Forecast models project future demand by product and location with confidence intervals. Forecast accuracy is measured against actual sales for continuous model improvement.

- **Supplier performance reports**: Vendor scorecards track on-time delivery, quantity accuracy, quality acceptance, and lead time consistency. Purchase price variance reports highlight cost changes requiring supplier negotiation or sourcing review.

- **Custom dashboards**: Drag-and-drop dashboard builders allow creation of role-specific views (warehouse manager, purchasing manager, CFO). Widgets include KPI cards, trend charts, exception lists, and data tables. Scheduled report delivery automates distribution to stakeholders.

### 12. Multi-Location Support

Multi-location capabilities manage distributed inventory operations:

- **Location hierarchy**: Organizations define unlimited warehouse and store locations with hierarchical roll-up for reporting. Virtual locations represent third-party logistics (3PL) providers, drop-ship suppliers, and in-transit positions. Location groups enable aggregate planning and reporting (e.g., all West Coast warehouses).

- **Distributed inventory visibility**: Real-time dashboard displays stock levels across all locations with drill-down to location detail. Location comparison reports identify stock imbalances and transfer opportunities. Enterprise-wide availability searches find inventory regardless of location.

- **Location-specific configuration**: Each location has independent reorder points, safety stocks, and valuation methods as appropriate. Location-specific units of measure support different stocking configurations. Location permissions control user access to sensitive inventory data.

- **Distributed order management**: Order routing rules assign orders to optimal fulfillment locations based on proximity, inventory availability, capacity, and cost. Split shipment logic determines when to fulfill from multiple locations versus waiting for consolidation. Backorder routing sources from alternative locations when primary locations are out of stock.

- **Inter-company transfers**: Multi-entity organizations handle transfers between legal entities with proper inter-company accounting. Transfer pricing rules ensure arms-length pricing for tax compliance. Cross-entity visibility respects security boundaries while enabling enterprise planning.

- **Consolidated reporting**: Financial and operational reports roll up across locations with currency conversion for international operations. Elimination entries remove inter-location transfers from consolidated results. Executive dashboards provide single-pane visibility across the enterprise.

## User Stories

### Story 1: Inventory Manager Adds New Product to Catalog

**As an** inventory manager, **I want to** add a new product to the catalog with complete specifications, variants, and stock locations, **so that** the product is available for purchase orders and sales orders.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Manager enters product name, description, SKU, and categorization.
- Manager configures product attributes (weight, dimensions, storage requirements).
- Manager creates size and color variants with unique SKUs for each combination.
- Manager assigns primary supplier with standard cost and reorder point.
- Manager defines units of measure (each, case, pallet) with conversion factors.
- Manager uploads product images and specification documents.
- Manager sets initial stock locations and quantities for each warehouse.
- System validates SKU uniqueness and required field completion.
- Product appears in catalog search and becomes available for ordering.

### Story 2: Warehouse Receives Purchase Order Shipment

**As a** warehouse receiving clerk, **I want to** receive a supplier shipment against a purchase order using barcode scanning, **so that** inventory is accurately recorded and available for fulfillment.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Clerk receives notification of expected delivery with PO number.
- Clerk scans PO barcode or selects from pending receipts list.
- System displays expected items with quantities and supplier details.
- Clerk scans product barcodes and enters received quantities.
- System captures lot numbers and expiry dates for tracked items.
- Clerk notes any damage or discrepancies with photo documentation.
- System updates on-hand inventory immediately upon confirmation.
- Put-away tasks are generated with suggested storage locations.
- Receiving documentation is archived for three-way matching.

### Story 3: Sales Orders are Picked and Shipped

**As a** warehouse picker, **I want to** pick sales orders efficiently using optimized pick lists and barcode scanning, **so that** orders are fulfilled accurately and on time.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- System generates pick lists organized by optimal pick path.
- Picker scans location barcode to confirm arrival at pick location.
- Picker scans product barcode to confirm correct item selection.
- System prompts for lot number or serial number capture as required.
- Picker enters picked quantity with validation against order quantity.
- System tracks pick progress with percentage complete and remaining items.
- Exceptions are flagged for stockouts or location discrepancies.
- Completed picks are staged for packing verification.
- Packing scan confirms complete order assembly before shipping.

### Story 4: Inventory Team Conducts Cycle Count

**As an** inventory control specialist, **I want to** conduct a cycle count of high-value items using mobile scanning, **so that** inventory accuracy is maintained and variances are identified promptly.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- System generates count schedule based on ABC classification.
- Specialist receives count task with location and expected quantity.
- Location is frozen for transactions during active count.
- Specialist scans location and product barcodes to record actual count.
- System calculates variance and flags significant discrepancies.
- Specialist enters count notes explaining observed conditions.
- Second count is required for variances exceeding threshold.

## Integration Points

### Flamingo Commerce Integration

The integration with Flamingo Commerce enables seamless product synchronization and order fulfillment:

- **Product sync**: Products created in Inventory automatically sync to Commerce for online selling. Inventory attributes map to commerce product specifications, and variant structures translate to online product options. Images and descriptions shared between systems ensure consistency.

- **Stock level sync**: Real-time inventory availability feeds into Commerce to prevent overselling. Available quantities consider allocated inventory, safety stock buffers, and pending orders. Stock updates publish to Commerce within seconds of inventory transactions.

- **Order fulfillment**: Orders placed in Commerce flow into Inventory as sales orders with automatic allocation and fulfillment workflow initiation. Order status updates (confirmed, picked, shipped) sync back to Commerce for customer notification. Tracking numbers generated in Inventory publish to Commerce for customer visibility.

- **Channel allocation**: Inventory can be allocated to specific commerce channels (web store, marketplace, wholesale portal) with channel-specific availability rules. Reserved inventory ensures high-priority channels have stock protection.

### Flamingo Procurement Integration

The integration with Flamingo Procurement coordinates purchasing workflows:

- **Requisition to PO**: Procurement requisitions approved in Procurement convert to purchase orders in Inventory with supplier, product, and quantity details preserved. Budget checking occurs in Procurement while inventory-specific validations (reorder points, lead times) apply in Inventory.

- **Supplier management**: Supplier master data is shared between systems with synchronized contact information, payment terms, and performance ratings. Supplier price lists maintained in Procurement feed into Inventory purchase order pricing.

- **Receipt coordination**: Purchase order receipts in Inventory update Procurement receiving records for three-way matching with supplier invoices. Discrepancies identified in Inventory receiving flag corresponding procurement records for resolution.

### Flamingo Books Integration

The integration with Flamingo Books ensures accurate financial tracking:

- **COGS posting**: Upon sales order shipment, Inventory calculates cost of goods sold using the selected valuation method and creates journal entries in Books. Debit to COGS account, credit to Inventory Asset account with dimensional tagging by product category and location.

- **Inventory valuation**: Period-end inventory valuations post to Books general ledger with reconciliation to physical inventory counts. Adjustments for obsolescence, damage, or count variances create corresponding journal entries.

- **Purchase accruals**: Received but not-yet-invoiced inventory creates accrual entries in Books for accurate month-end financials. Three-way matching completion triggers liability recognition and payment authorization.

- **Standard cost variance**: For standard cost valuation, purchase price variances post to variance accounts in Books for management analysis and standard cost update consideration.

### Flamingo ERP Integration

The integration with Flamingo ERP supports manufacturing and planning operations:

- **Bill of Materials**: BOMs defined in ERP synchronize to Inventory for production order material allocation and backflushing. Component requirements explode from finished goods production plans.

- **Production orders**: Production orders created in ERP generate material pick lists and planned finished goods receipts in Inventory. Component consumption and finished goods production update both systems.

- **MRP integration**: Material requirements planning in ERP considers on-hand and on-order inventory from Inventory to generate purchase and production recommendations. Planned orders from MRP convert to actual purchase orders and production orders.

- **Work in Progress**: WIP inventory tracking coordinates between shop floor reporting in ERP and inventory location management in Inventory. WIP moves between operations are tracked with quantity and location updates.

### Flamingo Payments Integration

The integration with Flamingo Payments handles supplier payment processing:

- **PO to payment**: Approved purchase orders with completed three-way matching flow to Payments for vendor payment scheduling. Payment terms from purchase orders determine payment timing.

- **Credit memo handling**: Supplier returns and credit memos in Inventory adjust payment obligations in Payments with reconciliation to bank transactions.

### Third-Party Integration

- **Carrier integration**: Shipping carriers (UPS, FedEx, USPS, DHL) integrate for rate shopping, label printing, and tracking. Shipment confirmations from carriers update order status automatically.

- **Marketplace integration**: APIs connect to Amazon, eBay, Walmart, and other marketplaces for inventory sync and order import. Inventory availability publishes to marketplaces, and marketplace orders import for fulfillment.

- **EDI integration**: Electronic data interchange with major retailers and suppliers supports 850 (purchase order), 856 (advance ship notice), and 810 (invoice) document exchange.

- **Barcode/RFID hardware**: Integration with Zebra, Honeywell, and other scanning devices for mobile warehouse operations. RFID reader integration supports automated inventory tracking.

## Technical Considerations

### Architecture

- **Backend**: Hono API server on Bun runtime with tRPC procedure layer for type-safe API contracts.
- **Database**: PostgreSQL 16 via Drizzle ORM with strict schema validation for inventory transactions.
- **Caching**: Redis caching for stock level queries with cache invalidation on inventory transactions.
- **Queue**: BullMQ for asynchronous operations including reorder calculations, demand forecasting, and report generation.
- **Real-time**: WebSocket connections for live stock level updates in dashboard and mobile applications.

### Concurrency and Consistency

- **Optimistic locking**: Inventory records use version fields to prevent lost updates during concurrent modifications.
- **Transaction isolation**: Inventory transactions use serializable isolation for quantity updates to prevent oversell conditions.
- **Distributed locking**: Distributed locks prevent simultaneous updates to the same product-location combination from multiple warehouse terminals.
- **Eventual consistency**: Cross-location and cross-system updates use eventual consistency patterns with reconciliation jobs for verification.

### Mobile and Scanning

- **Offline operation**: Mobile applications queue transactions locally for synchronization when connectivity is restored.
- **Barcode symbology**: Support for Code 128, Code 39, UPC, EAN, QR codes with camera scanning and Bluetooth scanner integration.
- **RFID integration**: RFID tag reading APIs for bulk inventory operations with tag write capabilities for encoding.

### Performance

- **Query optimization**: Stock level queries use materialized views and covering indexes for sub-100ms response times.
- **Batch processing**: Large inventory transactions (receipts, adjustments) use batch processing with progress tracking.
- **Report generation**: Complex reports execute asynchronously with email notification upon completion.

### Security

- **Role-based access**: Granular permissions control access to inventory data by location, category, and operation type.
- **Audit logging**: Immutable audit trail records all inventory transactions with user attribution and timestamp.
- **Data masking**: Cost information can be masked from users without financial permissions.

### Compliance

- **Lot traceability**: Complete forward and backward lot traceability supports FDA, USDA, and ISO compliance requirements.
- **Serialized tracking**: Serialized inventory supports warranty, recall, and regulatory serialization mandates.
- **Record retention**: Configurable retention policies ensure inventory records are maintained per regulatory requirements.
