# Flamingo Procurement - Sourcing and Purchasing Management

## Overview

Flamingo Procurement is a comprehensive sourcing and purchasing management module within the Flamingo financial ecosystem. It automates the entire procure-to-pay lifecycle, from initial requisition creation through vendor selection, purchase order management, receiving, and three-way matching. The module empowers organizations to control spending, streamline vendor relationships, optimize sourcing decisions, and maintain full visibility across all procurement activities.

The system encompasses requisition management with configurable approval workflows, vendor onboarding and performance tracking, strategic sourcing with RFP/RFQ capabilities, purchase order automation, contract lifecycle management, and spend analytics. Through native integration with Flamingo Inventory, purchase requisitions can automatically trigger when stock levels fall below thresholds. Integration with Flamingo Books ensures that every purchase order and receipt generates the appropriate accrual and liability entries, while three-way matching validates invoices before they reach accounts payable. Flamingo Spend provides budget visibility at the point of requisition, preventing overspending before commitments are made. Flamingo Sign enables digital contract execution with vendors, creating a complete audit trail from sourcing agreement to payment.

Flamingo Procurement targets mid-market and enterprise organizations that require structured purchasing controls, multi-level approvals, and strategic vendor management. The module supports complex organizational structures with department-level budgets, project-based purchasing, and global operations with multi-currency support. Organizations in manufacturing, retail, professional services, and healthcare benefit from the module's flexibility in handling both direct materials procurement and indirect spend management.

The core value proposition centers on four pillars: control (enforcing purchasing policies and budget compliance before money is committed), efficiency (reducing procurement cycle times by 60% through automation and self-service), visibility (providing real-time spend analytics and vendor performance metrics), and collaboration (enabling seamless communication between buyers, approvers, and suppliers through integrated portals).

The procurement software market is projected to exceed $15 billion by 2028, driven by digital transformation initiatives, supply chain disruption response, and increasing focus on spend optimization. Organizations are moving away from paper-based and email-driven procurement processes toward integrated, cloud-native solutions that provide end-to-end visibility and control.

## Core Features

### 1. Requisition Management

The requisition management system provides employees with an intuitive purchasing request experience while enforcing organizational controls:

- **Catalog purchasing**: Employees shop from approved catalogs with consumer-grade search, filtering, and product comparison. The system displays preferred items with contracted pricing, availability status, and estimated delivery dates. Shopping cart functionality allows multi-item requisitions with automatic splitting by vendor when necessary.
- **Non-catalog requests**: For items not in the catalog, employees submit free-form requisitions with item descriptions, specifications, estimated pricing, and suggested vendors. The system routes non-catalog requests through additional approval steps to ensure proper vendor selection and pricing validation.
- **Requisition templates**: Frequently purchased items can be saved as templates with pre-populated descriptions, categories, and vendor assignments. Templates support variable quantities and delivery dates while maintaining consistent specifications.
- **Mobile requisitioning**: Native mobile applications allow employees to create requisitions, attach photos of needed items, and submit for approval from any location. Push notifications alert employees to approval decisions and PO creation.
- **Requisition status tracking**: Employees can view the real-time status of their requisitions from submission through approval, PO creation, vendor acknowledgment, and receiving. Status updates trigger automatic notifications at each milestone.
- **Requisition modification**: Pending requisitions can be edited by the submitter. Approved requisitions require amendment workflows to modify quantities, delivery dates, or specifications. The system maintains full audit history of all changes.

### 2. Purchase Orders

The purchase order module automates PO creation, transmission, and lifecycle management:

- **Auto-PO generation**: Approved requisitions automatically generate purchase orders with pre-populated vendor, item, quantity, and pricing information. The system applies standard terms and conditions from the vendor master record.
- **PO templates**: Configurable PO templates support organizational branding, custom terms and conditions, and required fields by vendor or category. Templates ensure consistency and compliance with procurement policies.
- **Multi-vendor splitting**: Requisitions containing items from multiple vendors are automatically split into separate purchase orders by vendor. The system maintains cross-reference links between the original requisition and all resulting POs.
- **Transmission methods**: POs can be transmitted via email (PDF attachment), EDI (ANSI X12), cXML, supplier portal notification, or print/mail for vendors without electronic capabilities. The system tracks transmission status and confirms delivery.
- **PO acknowledgment**: Vendors acknowledge PO receipt and confirm acceptance or propose changes through the supplier portal or EDI responses. Acknowledgment status is visible to buyers with automated follow-up for non-responding vendors.
- **PO changes**: Purchase order amendments support quantity changes, delivery date updates, price adjustments, and cancellations. Change orders are transmitted to vendors with acknowledgment tracking and audit history.
- **PO closeout**: The system automatically closes POs when all items are received and invoiced. Manual closeout is available for canceled orders or partial fulfillments. Closed POs are archived with full transaction history.

### 3. Vendor Management

The vendor management system maintains comprehensive supplier information and performance tracking:

- **Vendor master data**: Centralized repository of vendor information including legal name, DBA, tax ID, remittance address, payment terms, currency, and primary contacts. Support for multiple remittance addresses and contact roles (sales, accounting, support).
- **Vendor onboarding**: Structured onboarding workflow collects required documentation (W-9/W-8, certificates of insurance, banking details for ACH), verifies tax ID validity, and conducts risk assessment screening. Onboarding checklists ensure compliance requirements are met before vendor activation.
- **Vendor classification**: Categorize vendors by type (materials, services, MRO, subcontractor), risk level (critical, high, medium, low), diversity status (minority-owned, women-owned, veteran-owned), and sustainability certifications.
- **Performance scorecards**: Configurable KPI tracking including on-time delivery percentage, quality acceptance rate, price competitiveness, and responsiveness metrics. Scorecards aggregate data from PO acknowledgments, receiving records, and quality inspections.
- **Vendor ratings**: Quarterly business review workflows with rating assignments across defined criteria. Rating history tracks vendor performance trends over time. Automated alerts flag vendors falling below performance thresholds.
- **Vendor segmentation**: ABC analysis categorizes vendors by spend volume and criticality. Strategic vendors receive enhanced relationship management while tactical vendors are managed through automated processes.
- **Vendor self-service**: Suppliers update their contact information, banking details, and certifications through the vendor portal. Changes are routed for internal approval before updating the master record.

### 4. Catalog Management

The catalog management system provides controlled access to approved products and pricing:

- **Hosted catalogs**: Internal catalogs maintained within the system with item descriptions, specifications, images, unit of measure, and contracted pricing. Support for multi-level category hierarchies and faceted search.
- **Punchout catalogs**: Integration with supplier e-commerce sites (cXML, OCI standards) allows employees to shop on supplier websites while maintaining purchasing controls. Selected items are returned to the procurement system for approval and PO generation.
- **Preferred item lists**: Flag frequently purchased items as preferred to promote their visibility in search results. Preferred items can be featured on the requisition homepage for one-click ordering.
- **Pricing management**: Contract pricing is maintained at the item-vendor level with effective dates and volume breaks. Price expiration alerts notify category managers of upcoming renewals. Historical price tracking identifies cost trends.
- **Catalog synchronization**: Automated updates from supplier price files or punchout sessions refresh catalog data. Change approval workflows ensure pricing updates are reviewed before becoming active.
- **Substitute items**: Define approved substitutes for items with limited availability. The system suggests substitutes during requisition when primary items are out of stock or discontinued.
- **Catalog analytics**: Usage reports show most-frequently-ordered items, catalog coverage gaps, and price variance against market benchmarks. Data drives catalog optimization decisions.

### 5. Contract Management

The contract lifecycle management system tracks agreements with vendors from creation through expiration:

- **Contract repository**: Centralized storage of all vendor contracts with full-text search, version control, and access permissions. Contracts can be linked to vendors, categories, and individual items.
- **Contract creation**: Templates for common contract types (MSA, SOW, pricing agreements) with clause libraries and standard terms. Integration with Flamingo Sign enables digital contract execution with full audit trails.
- **Obligation tracking**: Extract and track key obligations including delivery commitments, service levels, pricing tiers, and volume commitments. Calendar alerts notify stakeholders of upcoming obligations and deadlines.
- **Renewal management**: Automated alerts at configured intervals before contract expiration (90, 60, 30 days). Renewal workflows route contracts to stakeholders for review, renegotiation, or termination decisions.
- **Spend commitment tracking**: Monitor actual spend against contract commitments. Alerts notify buyers when approaching commitment thresholds. Penalty calculations for under-performance against minimum commitments.
- **Price protection**: Index-based pricing clauses automatically adjust contracted prices based on commodity indices or inflation measures. Historical price tracking validates adjustment calculations.
- **Contract amendments**: Amendment workflow tracks changes to contract terms, pricing, or scope. Amendment history is preserved with links to the original contract document.

### 6. Sourcing

The strategic sourcing module supports formal vendor selection and competitive bidding:

- **RFP/RFQ creation**: Structured request for proposal and request for quote documents with configurable sections (company overview, scope of work, technical requirements, commercial terms, evaluation criteria). Templates accelerate creation for common sourcing categories.
- **Supplier invitation**: Invite vendors to participate in sourcing events through the supplier portal or email. Track invitation acceptance and manage participant lists with confidentiality controls.
- **Bid submission**: Suppliers submit bids through a secure portal with form-based responses and attachment uploads. Blind bidding options prevent suppliers from seeing competitor pricing.
- **Bid comparison**: Side-by-side comparison matrices display responses across evaluation criteria. Total cost of ownership calculations incorporate freight, duties, payment terms, and lifecycle costs beyond unit pricing.
- **Scoring and evaluation**: Weighted scoring methodologies allow evaluation teams to rate bids across technical, commercial, and risk criteria. Score aggregation and normalization support consensus-based award decisions.
- **E-auctions**: Real-time reverse auction events where suppliers compete by lowering prices. Auction formats include English (ascending), Dutch (descending), and Japanese (simultaneous). Auto-extension rules prevent sniping.
- **Award management**: Document award decisions with justification narratives and approval workflows. Award notifications are sent to winning and losing bidders with feedback options for non-selected vendors.
- **Sourcing project tracking**: Pipeline view of all active sourcing initiatives with milestone tracking, savings validation, and project status. Integration with requisitioning allows employees to submit sourcing requests for new categories.

### 7. Three-Way Matching

The three-way matching system validates invoices against purchase orders and receipts before payment authorization:

- **Matching rules**: Configurable tolerance rules define acceptable variances between PO, receipt, and invoice quantities and prices. Rules can vary by vendor, category, or amount thresholds.
- **Automated matching**: System automatically matches invoices to POs and receipts when all three documents align within tolerance. Matched invoices flow directly to the payment queue without manual intervention.
- **Exception handling**: Invoices failing automatic matching are routed to exception queues with clear discrepancy indicators (quantity mismatch, price variance, missing receipt). Buyers can approve exceptions with documented justification.
- **Receipt verification**: Receiving module captures quantities, condition codes, and lot/serial numbers. Partial receipts are supported with automatic matching when final quantities are received.
- **Invoice receipt**: Multiple invoice ingestion methods including email capture, supplier portal upload, EDI, and manual entry. OCR extraction populates invoice header and line-item data.
- **Dispute management**: Structured workflow for invoice disputes with communication logging and resolution tracking. Dispute reasons are categorized for trend analysis.
- **Payment hold**: Ability to place payment holds on invoices with disputed quantities, quality issues, or contractual disputes. Hold release requires documented resolution and approval.

### 8. Spend Analysis

The spend analytics module provides comprehensive visibility into organizational purchasing:

- **Category analysis**: Spend categorized using standard taxonomies (UNSPSC, eCl@ss) or custom hierarchies. Category managers view spend distribution, vendor concentration, and price variance across categories.
- **Supplier spend**: Vendor-level spend analysis including purchase volume, transaction frequency, and payment terms utilization. Vendor concentration reports identify over-reliance on single suppliers.
- **Savings tracking**: Track realized savings from sourcing events, contract negotiations, and catalog optimization. Savings calculated against baseline pricing or previous contract terms.
- **Maverick spend**: Identify off-contract purchasing and non-compliant vendors. Reports show maverick spend by category, department, and employee with drill-down to specific transactions.
- **Price variance**: Compare actual paid prices against contracted prices, market indices, and peer pricing. Variance alerts notify buyers of potential overpayment.
- **Trend analysis**: Time-series analysis of spend patterns with seasonality detection and forecasting. Compare current period spend against prior periods and budgets.
- **Custom dashboards**: Drag-and-drop dashboard builder for procurement KPIs including cost per PO, cycle time, on-time delivery, and contract coverage percentage.

### 9. Approval Workflows

The approval workflow engine enforces organizational purchasing policies through configurable routing:

- **Multi-level approvals**: Configure up to 10 approval levels with different approvers at each stage. Each level can have multiple approvers with configurable logic (all must approve, any one can approve, majority approval).
- **Conditional routing**: Route requisitions based on amount, category, department, vendor, project, or custom fields. Conditions support AND/OR logic for complex routing rules.
- **Approval thresholds**: Auto-approve requisitions below configured thresholds. Thresholds can vary by category, employee role, or budget availability.
- **Delegate approvers**: Approvers can designate delegates for periods of absence. Delegates have full approval authority with audit logging of both original approver and delegate.
- **Approval reminders**: Configurable reminder notifications for pending approvals with escalation rules for overdue items. Approvals pending beyond deadlines automatically escalate to the next level or designated escalation contact.
- **Approval comments**: Approvers can add comments to approval actions, request additional information, or flag items for review. Comments are visible to submitters and subsequent approvers.
- **Approval delegation**: Temporary delegation rules allow automatic rerouting when approvers are out of office. Delegation periods are configurable with automatic restoration.

### 10. Budget Checking

The budget validation system prevents overspending through pre-commitment checking:

- **Pre-purchase validation**: Requisitions are validated against available budgets before approval. Budgets can be defined by department, project, cost center, category, or GL account.
- **Encumbrance accounting**: Approved requisitions encumber (reserve) budget funds, reducing available balance for subsequent requests. Encumbrances are released when POs are issued or requisitions are canceled.
- **Budget overrides**: Configurable override rules allow certain roles to approve over-budget requisitions with documented justification. Override authority can be limited by amount or category.
- **Real-time visibility**: Requisitioners and approvers see real-time budget status including committed, encumbered, and available amounts. Budget alerts warn when available funds fall below thresholds.
- **Budget integration**: Budget data is synchronized from Flamingo Spend with support for multiple budget versions (original, revised, forecast). Budget transfers and adjustments are reflected in real-time.
- **Soft and hard stops**: Configurable enforcement modes allow warnings (soft stop) or blocking (hard stop) when budgets are exceeded. Soft stops require acknowledgment while hard stops prevent submission.

### 11. Supplier Portal

The supplier portal provides vendors with self-service access to relevant procurement information:

- **PO visibility**: Suppliers view all purchase orders issued to them with line-item details, delivery requirements, and terms. PO status indicates whether acknowledgment is pending or confirmed.
- **PO acknowledgment**: Vendors acknowledge PO receipt and confirm acceptance or propose changes to quantities, pricing, or delivery dates. Acknowledgment data flows back to the buyer's procurement system.
- **Advanced ship notices**: Suppliers create ASNs with shipment details including tracking numbers, carrier, and expected delivery date. ASN data pre-populates receiving records for faster goods receipt.
- **Invoice submission**: Vendors submit invoices through the portal with attachment upload or EDI integration. Invoices are automatically matched to POs and routed for approval.
- **Payment status**: Suppliers view payment status for submitted invoices including approval status, scheduled payment date, and remittance details. Payment history is available for reconciliation.
- **Performance feedback**: Suppliers view their performance scorecards and rating history. Feedback forms allow buyers to share improvement opportunities.
- **Document exchange**: Secure document sharing for contracts, specifications, quality documentation, and compliance certificates. Version control tracks document updates.

### 12. Reporting

The reporting module provides procurement metrics and analytics for stakeholders:

- **Procurement KPIs**: Standard reports for key metrics including spend under management, cost savings, supplier count, PO cycle time, and contract coverage. Metrics trended over time with target comparisons.
- **Supplier performance**: Reports on vendor on-time delivery, quality metrics, and responsiveness. Performance trended by vendor and aggregated by category.
- **Savings validation**: Documented savings from sourcing initiatives, contract negotiations, and process improvements. Savings categorized by type (hard savings, cost avoidance, efficiency gains).
- **Compliance reporting**: Policy compliance metrics including on-contract percentage, approval compliance, and maverick spend identification. Compliance by department and category.
- **Operational reports**: Transaction-level reports for POs, receipts, invoices, and requisitions. Reports support filtering by date range, vendor, department, and status.
- **Executive dashboards**: High-level summaries for leadership including total spend, savings achieved, top vendors, and procurement team productivity.
- **Scheduled delivery**: Reports can be scheduled for automatic generation and email delivery on daily, weekly, or monthly intervals.

## User Stories

### 1. Employee Creates Purchase Requisition

**As an** employee who needs to purchase supplies for my department, **I want to** create a purchase requisition through the catalog **so that** I can obtain the items I need while following company purchasing procedures.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Employee searches the catalog using keywords or browses by category
- System displays preferred items with contracted pricing and availability
- Employee adds items to cart with quantity and required delivery date
- System validates the selected vendor is approved and items are on contract
- Employee adds business justification and selects charge account (department/project)
- System checks budget availability and displays remaining funds
- Employee submits requisition and receives confirmation with tracking number
- Approver receives notification with requisition details and approval link

### 2. Manager Approves Team Requisitions

**As a** department manager, **I want to** review and approve purchase requisitions from my team **so that** I can ensure purchases align with department needs and budget constraints.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Manager receives email notification when direct reports submit requisitions
- Approval queue displays pending requisitions with item details, amounts, and business justifications
- Manager can view linked budget impact including current spend and available funds
- Manager approves, rejects, or requests additional information with comments
- Approved requisitions automatically generate purchase orders
- Rejected requisitions return to submitter with explanation
- Manager can delegate approval authority during absence
- Approval history is maintained for audit purposes

### 3. Procurement Specialist Runs Sourcing Event

**As a** procurement specialist, **I want to** create and manage an RFQ for a new service category **so that** I can select the best vendor based on competitive bidding.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Specialist creates RFQ with scope of work, evaluation criteria, and timeline
- System suggests potential vendors from the approved supplier database
- Invitations are sent to selected vendors through the supplier portal
- Vendors submit bids with pricing and technical responses
- System generates side-by-side comparison matrix for bid evaluation
- Evaluation team scores bids across weighted criteria
- Award decision is documented with justification narrative
- Winning vendor is notified and contract creation workflow is initiated

### 4. Buyer Processes Three-Way Match Exceptions

**As a** procurement buyer, **I want to** review and resolve invoice matching exceptions **so that** valid invoices are approved for payment while discrepancies are investigated.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Exception queue displays invoices failing automatic matching with discrepancy details
- System shows PO quantity/price, received quantity, and invoiced quantity/price
- Buyer can approve price variances within authorized tolerance
- Buyer can request corrected invoices from vendors for significant discrepancies
- Approved exceptions flow to accounts payable for payment processing
- Rejected invoices are returned to vendor with explanation
- Resolution actions are logged with timestamp and approver identity

### 5. Vendor Maintains Profile Through Portal

**As a** vendor account manager, **I want to** update my company's contact information and banking details through the supplier portal **so that** our customer has current information for PO transmission and payment.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Vendor logs into secure portal with provided credentials
- Vendor views and updates primary contact information and addresses
- Vendor submits banking details for ACH payment enrollment
- Changes are routed to buyer for review and approval
- Vendor views all active purchase orders and acknowledgment status
- Vendor submits invoices against approved POs with attachment upload
- Vendor views payment status and remittance history

### 6. Finance Director Analyzes Department Spend

**As a** finance director, **I want to** analyze spending patterns across departments and categories **so that** I can identify cost reduction opportunities and budget variances.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Director accesses spend analytics dashboard with summary KPIs
- Spend can be filtered by department, category, vendor, and time period
- System displays trend charts showing spend patterns over time
- Category analysis identifies top spending categories and vendor concentration
- Maverick spend report shows off-contract purchasing by department
- Savings tracking report validates procurement team value delivery
- Reports can be exported to Excel and PDF for presentation

### 7. Contract Manager Tracks Agreement Renewals

**As a** contract manager, **I want to** track upcoming contract expirations and manage renewal workflows **so that** critical vendor agreements are renewed without service interruption.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Dashboard displays contracts expiring in next 90, 60, and 30 days

- Manager initiates renewal workflow with review and renegotiation steps
- Contract documents are linked to renewal workflow for reference
- Stakeholders receive tasks for contract review and approval
- Renewal decisions (renew, renegotiate, terminate) are documented

**As a** system administrator, **I want to** configure multi-level approval workflows with conditional routing **so that** requisitions are routed to appropriate approvers based on organizational policies.

## Integration Points

### Inventory Integration

The integration with Flamingo Inventory enables demand-driven procurement:

- **Stock level monitoring**: Inventory levels are monitored against reorder points and safety stock thresholds. When inventory falls below thresholds, automatic requisitions are created for replenishment.
- **PO-to-receipt flow**: Purchase orders flow seamlessly to receiving. When goods are received against POs, inventory quantities are automatically updated with lot/serial tracking as applicable.
- **Backorder management**: Partial receipts update inventory availability while maintaining backorder visibility for remaining quantities.
- **Demand forecasting**: Historical consumption data from Inventory feeds into procurement planning to suggest optimal reorder quantities and timing.
- **Inventory valuation**: Receipt transactions create inventory asset entries in Flamingo Books with proper valuation methods (FIFO, LIFO, weighted average).

### Books Integration

The integration with Flamingo Books ensures accurate financial recording:

- **PO accruals**: When purchase orders are issued, the system can create commitment or encumbrance entries depending on organizational accounting policies.
- **Receipt accruals**: Goods receipts create accrued liability entries (debit inventory/expense, credit accrued liabilities) before invoices are received.
- **Invoice posting**: Approved invoices from the three-way matching process flow to accounts payable in Books with proper GL coding, dimensional tagging, and vendor credits.
- **Payment processing**: Payment approvals in Procurement trigger payment execution in Flamingo Payments with automatic cash application.
- **Period close**: Procurement transactions respect Books period close status, preventing posting to closed periods.

### ERP Integration

The integration with Flamingo ERP provides enterprise-level data synchronization:

- **Master data sync**: Vendor master data, item master data, and chart of accounts are synchronized between ERP and Procurement to ensure consistency.
- **Demand planning**: Material requirements from ERP planning modules flow into Procurement as requisitions or planned orders.
- **Work order integration**: Direct material purchases for manufacturing work orders are linked to ERP work orders for cost rollup.
- **Asset integration**: Capital purchases flow to the ERP asset module for depreciation scheduling and asset tracking.
- **Intercompany purchasing**: Intercompany transactions are properly flagged and routed for consolidated reporting.

### Spend Integration

The integration with Flamingo Spend provides budget control:

- **Budget validation**: Requisitions are validated against Spend budgets in real-time before approval. Budgets can be defined by department, project, cost center, or category.
- **Encumbrance tracking**: Approved requisitions encumber budget funds, reducing available balances for future requests.
- **Actuals feed**: Purchase orders, receipts, and invoices feed actual spend data into Spend for budget vs. actual reporting.
- **Forecasting**: Procurement pipeline data (approved requisitions not yet invoiced) feeds into Spend forecasting for cash flow projection.
- **Variance alerts**: When procurement activity approaches or exceeds budget thresholds, alerts are triggered in both systems.

### Sign Integration

The integration with Flamingo Sign enables digital contract execution:

- **Contract creation**: Vendor agreements created in Procurement can be sent for electronic signature through Sign with proper routing and approval chains.
- **Template integration**: Procurement contract templates integrate with Sign templates for consistent formatting and clause inclusion.
- **Execution tracking**: Signature status is visible within the Procurement contract record with automated notifications at each signing milestone.
- **Document storage**: Executed contracts are automatically stored in the Procurement document repository with version control and access permissions.
- **Renewal workflows**: Contract expiration alerts in Procurement trigger renewal workflows that may include re-execution through Sign.

## Technical Considerations

### Punchout Catalog Integration

Punchout catalog integration requires cXML or OCI (Open Catalog Interface) support:

- **cXML standard**: Support for cXML 1.2.036 and later for punchout setup request, punchout order message, and order response.
- **OCI standard**: Support for OCI 4.0 and 5.0 for HTML form-based punchout integration with SAP and other ERP systems.
- **Roundtrip handling**: User authentication tokens must be passed to supplier sites and returned with shopping cart data to maintain session continuity.
- **Price synchronization**: Hosted catalogs require regular price updates via supplier feed files (CSV, XML) or API connections.
- **Catalog caching**: Product data should be cached locally for search performance while maintaining synchronization with source systems.

### EDI Support

Electronic Data Interchange enables automated business document exchange:

- **X12 standards**: Support for ANSI X12 EDI documents including 850 (Purchase Order), 855 (PO Acknowledgment), 856 (ASN), 810 (Invoice), and 820 (Payment Order).
- **EDIFACT support**: Support for EDIFACT messages for international suppliers including ORDERS, ORDRSP, DESADV, and INVOIC.
- **VAN integration**: Integration with Value Added Networks (VANs) for EDI transmission or direct AS2 connections for larger trading partners.
- **Document mapping**: Configurable mapping between internal data formats and EDI segments/elements to accommodate trading partner-specific requirements.
- **Error handling**: EDI syntax validation, functional acknowledgment (997/CONTRL) processing, and exception routing for failed transmissions.

### Supplier Data Synchronization

Supplier master data requires careful synchronization:

- **Data validation**: Tax ID validation against IRS TIN matching service, bank account validation via micro-deposits or account verification services.
- **Compliance screening**: Integration with third-party screening services for OFAC, sanctions list, and anti-money laundering checks.
- **Diversity certification**: Integration with certifying agency databases (NMSDC, WBENC, NVBDC) to validate diversity status.
- **Document expiration**: Automated monitoring of insurance certificates, certifications, and contracts with expiration alerts.
- **Change management**: Supplier-initiated changes through the portal require approval workflows before updating master data.

### Performance Considerations

- **Catalog search**: Full-text search across catalog items should return results in under 2 seconds for catalogs with 100,000+ items. Consider Elasticsearch or similar search technology.
- **Spend analytics**: Aggregation queries across millions of transactions should complete in under 5 seconds. Implement materialized views or OLAP cubes for reporting.
- **Matching engine**: Three-way matching should process invoices in near-real-time to avoid payment delays. Batch processing for high-volume scenarios.
- **Concurrent users**: Support for 1,000+ concurrent users creating requisitions during peak periods without performance degradation.
