# Flamingo Payments - Global Payment Gateway and Transactions Module

## Overview

Flamingo Payments is a comprehensive global payment processing platform that empowers businesses of all sizes to accept payments from customers anywhere in the world. As the central payment infrastructure within the Flamingo ecosystem, Payments provides a unified gateway for processing transactions across multiple channels, currencies, and payment methods while maintaining the highest standards of security, compliance, and developer experience.

The platform serves as the financial backbone that connects all other Flamingo products to the global payments ecosystem. Whether processing e-commerce transactions through Flamingo Commerce, collecting invoice payments via Flamingo Invoice, handling recurring subscription billing through Flamingo Billing, or managing payroll disbursements via Flamingo Payroll, Flamingo Payments provides the secure, reliable transaction processing that makes these workflows possible. Every payment flows through a unified infrastructure that ensures consistency, compliance, and comprehensive reporting.

Flamingo Payments supports businesses ranging from solopreneurs and startups to enterprise organizations with complex multi-entity structures and high transaction volumes. The platform's architecture handles millions of transactions daily across 135+ currencies and 40+ countries, with automatic regulatory compliance, intelligent fraud prevention, and real-time reconciliation. Whether accepting card payments from local customers, processing bank transfers from international clients, or managing marketplace payouts to vendors, businesses benefit from a single, integrated payment platform that scales with their growth.

The core value proposition centers on four pillars: global reach (accepting payments in any currency through local payment methods), unified infrastructure (one integration for all payment needs across the Flamingo ecosystem), intelligent protection (machine learning-powered fraud prevention and dispute management), and financial control (comprehensive reporting, reconciliation, and payout management). By combining these capabilities with seamless integration into the broader Flamingo financial ecosystem, Flamingo Payments eliminates the complexity of managing multiple payment providers while providing enterprise-grade capabilities.

## Market Analysis

### Market Landscape

The global payment processing market represents one of the most dynamic and rapidly evolving sectors in financial technology, with transaction volumes exceeding trillions of dollars annually. The market is driven by the continued shift toward digital commerce, the proliferation of mobile payments, the expansion of cross-border trade, and the emergence of new payment methods from digital wallets to buy-now-pay-later services. Businesses increasingly demand payment infrastructure that can handle complex scenarios—subscriptions, marketplaces, multi-currency operations, and platform payments—through a unified interface rather than stitching together multiple specialized providers.

Key market trends shaping payment platform development include:

- **Embedded payments**: The integration of payment processing directly into business software and workflows, eliminating the need for separate payment portals or manual reconciliation between systems.
- **Global localization**: Support for local payment methods, currencies, and compliance requirements in every market where businesses operate, rather than forcing international customers to use unfamiliar payment methods.
- **Real-time capabilities**: Instant payment authorization, immediate settlement options, and real-time fraud decisioning that reduces friction while maintaining security.
- **Developer experience**: Clean APIs, comprehensive documentation, and robust SDKs that enable developers to integrate payment capabilities quickly and confidently.
- **Intelligent automation**: Machine learning for fraud detection, smart routing for approval optimization, and automated reconciliation that reduces manual financial operations.

## Core Features

### 1. Payment Methods

Flamingo Payments supports a comprehensive array of payment methods to accommodate customer preferences across markets and use cases:

**Credit and Debit Cards**: Full support for major card networks including Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover, Diners Club, JCB, and UnionPay. Card processing includes support for consumer credit and debit cards, commercial cards, prepaid cards, and virtual cards. The platform handles EMV chip authentication, magnetic stripe transactions, and contactless/NFC payments for in-person scenarios. Dynamic currency conversion enables international cardholders to pay in their home currency at the point of sale.

**Bank Transfers and ACH**: Automated Clearing House (ACH) transactions for US-based bank transfers with support for both debit (pulling funds from customer accounts) and credit (pushing funds to vendor accounts) transactions. Same-day ACH processing provides faster settlement for time-sensitive payments. The platform supports ACH micro-deposit verification for bank account validation and instant bank account verification through partnerships with financial data providers. International bank transfer support includes SEPA for Eurozone countries, BACS for the United Kingdom, and wire transfer capabilities for high-value transactions.

**Digital Wallets**: Native support for Apple Pay, Google Pay, Samsung Pay, and PayPal enables one-click payment experiences on web and mobile. Digital wallet integration includes automatic device detection, appropriate button display, and streamlined authentication through biometric verification or device PIN. Wallet payments tokenize card data for enhanced security while maintaining compatibility with existing card processing infrastructure. Support for emerging wallets including Amazon Pay, Venmo, and regional wallets expands customer payment options.

**Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL)**: Integration with leading BNPL providers including Klarna, Afterpay, Affirm, and Sezzle enables customers to split purchases into interest-free installments while merchants receive full payment upfront. BNPL options are presented dynamically based on transaction amount, customer location, and merchant configuration. The platform handles BNPL transaction routing, status updates, and reconciliation with merchant reporting showing both gross transaction amounts and BNPL-specific fees.

**Local Payment Methods**: Region-specific payment methods are automatically offered based on customer location, including iDEAL (Netherlands), Bancontact (Belgium), giropay (Germany), EPS (Austria), Przelewy24 (Poland), Sofort/Klarna (multiple European countries), Interac (Canada), and FPX (Malaysia). Local method support includes proper language localization, bank selection interfaces, and transaction status tracking that accounts for the asynchronous nature of many bank transfer methods.

**Alternative Payments**: Support for cryptocurrency payments through integration with crypto payment processors, enabling acceptance of Bitcoin, Ethereum, and stablecoins with automatic conversion to fiat currency. Cash payment networks allow customers to pay with cash at retail locations for online purchases. Mobile money integration supports M-Pesa and similar systems popular in African and Asian markets. Gift card and store credit processing enables closed-loop payment systems for merchant-specific currency.

### 3. Payment Intents

The Payment Intents API provides comprehensive control over the transaction lifecycle:

**Authorization and Capture**: The two-step payment flow authorizes funds on the customer's payment method without immediately charging, enabling order verification, fraud review, or inventory confirmation before final capture. Authorization holds typically last 7-10 days depending on the payment method. Partial captures allow charging less than the authorized amount, with remaining authorization automatically released. Multiple partial captures against a single authorization support split shipments or partial fulfillment scenarios.

**Automatic Capture**: Configurable automatic capture rules can capture authorized funds after a specified time period, upon order status changes from integrated Flamingo products, or when fulfillment events occur. Automatic capture reduces manual intervention while ensuring appropriate verification periods. Merchants can override automatic capture on a per-transaction basis for exceptional cases requiring manual review.

**Refunds and Returns**: Full and partial refund capabilities support all payment methods with automatic calculation of refundable amounts based on transaction history. Refunds can be issued back to the original payment method or to alternative methods when original methods are unavailable. Refund timing varies by payment method—immediate for digital wallets, 5-10 business days for card refunds, and method-dependent for bank transfers. Refund reason codes and notes support reporting and analytics.

**Voids and Cancellations**: Uncaptured authorizations can be voided to release held funds immediately, which is preferable to capture-then-refund for cancelled orders. Voids are processed in real-time for most payment methods. Authorization expiration handling automatically releases holds when authorizations expire without capture, with configurable notifications for expiring authorizations.

**Disputed Transaction Reversals**: Chargeback and dispute handling includes automatic reversal of disputed amounts, evidence submission workflows, and representment capabilities for fighting invalid disputes. Dispute notifications trigger alerts in merchant dashboards with deadline tracking for response requirements. Dispute analytics identify patterns and provide recommendations for reducing future disputes.

**Idempotency and Reliability**: All payment operations support idempotency keys to prevent duplicate charges during network failures or retry scenarios. The platform guarantees exactly-once processing for payment operations, ensuring customers are never charged multiple times due to technical issues. Automatic retry logic with exponential backoff handles transient failures while preventing unnecessary decline costs.

### 6. Fraud Prevention

Comprehensive fraud protection safeguards businesses and customers:

**Radar Machine Learning**: The fraud detection engine uses machine learning models trained on millions of transactions to score each payment for risk. Models consider hundreds of signals including device fingerprinting, behavioral biometrics, transaction patterns, velocity checks, and consortium data. Risk scores enable automated decisioning with customizable thresholds for blocking, reviewing, or accepting transactions.

**Custom Rules Engine**: Merchants can create custom fraud rules based on transaction attributes, customer behavior, and business-specific risk factors. Rules can block transactions, request additional verification, or flag for manual review. Rule testing enables simulation against historical data before deployment. A/B testing of fraud strategies optimizes the balance between blocking fraud and minimizing false declines.

**3D Secure Authentication**: Support for 3D Secure 2.0 adds an additional authentication layer for card transactions, shifting liability for fraudulent chargebacks to card issuers while maintaining frictionless checkout experiences. 3D Secure can be required for all transactions, high-risk transactions, transactions exceeding thresholds, or transactions from specific regions. Frictionless flows authenticate low-risk transactions without customer intervention.

**Address and Identity Verification**: AVS (Address Verification Service) checks billing address against card issuer records. CVV verification confirms the security code matches card records. Identity verification services validate customer identity through document verification, biometric matching, and database lookups for high-risk scenarios.

**Velocity and Pattern Detection**: Velocity checks identify unusual transaction patterns including rapid-fire attempts, multiple cards from single devices, and transactions from high-risk IP addresses. Pattern detection identifies behavioral anomalies like unusual purchase timing, atypical product combinations, or suspicious browsing patterns prior to purchase.

**Dispute Prediction**: Machine learning models predict the likelihood of future disputes based on transaction characteristics, enabling proactive intervention before chargebacks occur. High dispute-risk transactions can trigger additional verification, customer communication, or fulfillment delays to prevent losses.

**Blacklist and Whitelist Management**: Block lists prevent transactions from known bad actors, suspicious devices, or compromised payment methods. Allow lists ensure trusted customers experience minimal friction. Lists can be managed manually or populated automatically based on transaction outcomes.

Chargeback ratio tracking ensures compliance with card network limits (typically 1% of transactions). Trend analysis predicts future dispute volumes and identifies emerging fraud patterns.

**Prevention Alerts**: Early warning systems from card networks alert merchants to customer complaints before they become chargebacks, enabling proactive refunds or customer service intervention. Prevention alerts can automatically issue refunds to avoid chargeback fees and ratio impacts.

**Compliance Monitoring**: Automated monitoring ensures dispute ratios remain below card network thresholds that trigger compliance programs. Program enrollment guidance helps merchants navigate remediation requirements if thresholds are exceeded. Compliance documentation and reporting supports merchant account health.

### 8. Reconciliation

High-confidence matches are reconciled automatically while exceptions queue for manual review. Match confidence scoring helps prioritize manual review efforts.

**Payout Reconciliation**: Settlement reports from payment processors are automatically matched to bank account deposits, identifying discrepancies in amounts, timing, or missing deposits. Fee extraction separates processing fees from gross transaction amounts for proper expense recognition. Multi-currency settlement reconciliation handles exchange rate differences.

**Discrepancy Investigation**: Exception handling workflows investigate unreconciled transactions including missing deposits, amount mismatches, and timing differences. Discrepancy categorization identifies systematic issues requiring process changes versus isolated errors. Audit trails document investigation steps and resolution actions.

**Financial Reporting**: Reconciliation reports provide cleared transaction lists, outstanding items, and reconciliation summaries for accounting period close. Deposit forecasting predicts upcoming settlement amounts based on pending transactions and historical patterns. Cash flow reporting integrates reconciliation data with accounts receivable and payable.

**Automated Adjustments**: Minor discrepancies within configurable tolerances can be automatically adjusted with journal entries for rounding differences, exchange rate fluctuations, or fee allocation adjustments. Adjustment rules ensure consistent treatment of common reconciliation variances.

**Bank Feed Integration**: Direct connections to thousands of financial institutions enable automatic import of bank transactions for matching. Bank feeds support checking accounts, savings accounts, and merchant accounts across major banks. Transaction categorization learns from historical patterns to improve matching accuracy.

## Technical Considerations

### PCI DSS Compliance

Flamingo Payments maintains PCI DSS Level 1 compliance, the highest level of payment security certification:

**Tokenization Architecture**: Sensitive card data never touches merchant servers—card numbers are immediately tokenized upon entry, with only non-sensitive tokens passing through merchant systems. Tokenization prevents card data exposure in merchant databases, logs, and memory.

**Hosted Fields and iframes**: Card data entry occurs in PCI-compliant hosted fields or iframe elements that isolate sensitive input from merchant pages. This architecture enables custom checkout designs while maintaining compliance scope reduction.

**Encryption Standards**: All data transmission uses TLS 1.3 encryption with strong cipher suites. Data at rest uses AES-256 encryption with hardware security module (HSM) key management. Encryption keys rotate automatically with secure key escrow procedures.

**Compliance Validation**: Annual QSA (Qualified Security Assessor) audits validate PCI DSS compliance. Quarterly network scans by ASVs (Approved Scanning Vendors) identify vulnerabilities. Attestation of Compliance (AOC) documentation available for merchant acquirer relationships.

**Merchant Scope Reduction**: By using Flamingo Payments' tokenization and hosted fields, merchants can typically achieve SAQ A or SAQ A-EP compliance levels rather than more complex SAQ D, significantly reducing compliance burden and cost.

### Tokenization and Data Security

**Token Vault**: The token vault stores sensitive payment method data with strict access controls, encryption, and audit logging. Tokens are format-preserving where possible (matching card number patterns) to minimize application changes.

**Network Tokens**: Support for card network tokenization (Visa Token Service, Mastercard Digital Enablement) provides additional security layers with dynamic cryptograms for each transaction, reducing fraud and improving approval rates.

**Secure Authentication**: API authentication uses API keys with granular permission scopes, mutual TLS for server-to-server communication, and OAuth 2.0 for delegated access scenarios. Key rotation procedures ensure ongoing security.

**Data Retention Policies**: Configurable data retention policies automatically purge sensitive data according to business requirements and regulatory obligations. Data deletion includes cryptographic wiping and certificate revocation.

### Idempotency and Reliability

**Exactly-Once Processing**: Payment operations guarantee exactly-once execution through idempotency key tracking. Duplicate requests with the same idempotency key return the original response without reprocessing, preventing double charges during network retries.

**Retry Logic**: Automatic retry with exponential backoff handles transient failures including network timeouts, rate limiting, and temporary processor unavailability. Retries are idempotent-safe and include circuit breaker patterns to prevent cascading failures.

**Webhook Reliability**: Webhook delivery includes automatic retries with exponential backoff, dead letter queues for failed deliveries, and delivery confirmation tracking. Webhook signatures enable recipient verification of authenticity.

**Disaster Recovery**: Multi-region deployment with automatic failover ensures payment processing continuity during infrastructure failures. Recovery Time Objective (RTO) of 5 minutes and Recovery Point Objective (RPO) of near-zero for transaction data.

**Transaction Integrity**: Database transactions ensure ACID properties for all payment operations. Two-phase commit protocols maintain consistency across distributed systems. Reconciliation processes detect and resolve any consistency anomalies.

### Performance and Scalability

**High Throughput**: Architecture supports 10,000+ transactions per second with horizontal scaling capabilities. Load balancing distributes traffic across processing nodes with health-based routing.

**Low Latency**: API response times target <200ms for authorization requests with global CDN distribution minimizing network latency. Edge processing handles validation and routing decisions close to request sources.

**Caching Strategy**: Multi-tier caching including edge caching for configuration data, Redis for session and token data, and application caching for frequently accessed records. Cache invalidation is event-driven for consistency.

**Database Optimization**: Read replicas handle reporting and analytics queries while primary databases handle transactional writes. Connection pooling and query optimization ensure efficient database utilization.

### Monitoring and Observability

**Real-Time Monitoring**: Comprehensive monitoring tracks transaction volumes, approval rates, error rates, and latency percentiles. Alerting notifies operations teams of anomalies including volume spikes, decline rate increases, or latency degradation.

**Distributed Tracing**: End-to-end request tracing follows transactions through all system components, enabling performance analysis and troubleshooting. Trace IDs correlate logs across services for holistic visibility.

**Audit Logging**: Immutable audit logs capture all payment operations with user attribution, timestamps, and change details. Logs are tamper-evident with cryptographic verification and long-term archival.

**Business Intelligence**: Real-time dashboards and scheduled reports provide visibility into payment performance, fraud trends, and operational metrics. Data warehouse integration enables custom analytics and machine learning.
